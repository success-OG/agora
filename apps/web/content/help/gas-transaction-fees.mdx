# Gas & Transaction Fees on Stellar

When you use blockchain networks like Stellar, every transaction (such as buying a ticket or creating an event) requires a small fee. This fee is often referred to as "gas."

## What is a Transaction Fee?

A transaction fee prevents the network from being spammed with empty or malicious transactions. It compensates the network for processing and verifying your action.

## Fees on the Stellar Network

One of the biggest advantages of the Stellar network is that its fees are incredibly low compared to other blockchains. 

- **Base Fee:** The minimum fee on Stellar is just **100 stroops** (0.00001 XLM).
- **In USD:** At typical market prices, this equates to a tiny fraction of a cent per transaction.

## How Do I Pay Fees?

Fees on the Stellar network are paid in **XLM** (Stellar Lumens), the native currency of the network. 

**Even if you are purchasing a ticket with USDC, you must have a small amount of XLM in your wallet to cover the transaction fee.**

### Step-by-Step Summary

1. **Keep XLM for Fees:** Always keep at least 2-3 XLM in your wallet. This covers the network's minimum reserve requirement and provides enough "gas" for thousands of transactions.
2. **Review Before Signing:** When you buy a ticket on Agora, your wallet will show you the exact XLM fee being charged before you approve the transaction.
3. **Approve:** Once you are comfortable with the fee, sign the transaction.

For more technical details on how fees work, you can visit the [Stellar Developer Documentation](https://developers.stellar.org/docs/encyclopedia/fees-surge-pricing-fee-strategies).
